Mogao Grotto No. 420（Sui Dynasty）

Views :
Update time : 2026-02-27

The cave was founded in the Sui Dynasty, and some murals were repainted in the Song and Western Xia Dynasties. The main room has a bucket-shaped roof, and the south, west and north walls each have a niche, which is called the "three niche caves". The top of the cave in the main room is a four lotus algae well, decorated with three rabbit patterns in the centre of the well. The inner corner of the algae well is painted in the shape of a boy flying in the sky, the outer part is painted with a winged beast, and the outer frame of the square well is decorated with a winter lion bead pattern. The hanging mantles on all sides are laid on the four sides. There is a large-scale painting of the meridians. Each scene is separated by trees, stones and flowers, pagoda temples and temples, flowing spring lotus ponds, flowing clouds and flying flowers and other scenes. However, due to the dense and complicated pictures of the four paintings, coupled with the serious discolouration, some of the pictures are very difficult to read. Therefore, there is also a debate in the academic community about the understanding of the content of the four paintings of the cave, which are mainly divided into "Fahua Sutra Change Theory" and "Fahua Sutra Change and Nirvana Sutra Change Theory". This explanation is subject to the "Fahuajing Change". On the west wall, there is a square round niche inside and outside, and a sitting Buddha, two disciples and four Bodhisattvas are sculpted in the niche. The upper part of the north and south sides of the outside of the shrine is painted with the Wei Maji Sutra, the south side is Manjushri Bodhisattva, the north side is the Wei Maji, and the lower part on both sides is painted with five disciples and four Bodhisattvas. In the lower part of the west wall, the Song Dynasty painted offerings and offerings to Bodhisattva. In the centre of the south wall and the north wall, there is a square shallow niche, in which there are two sitting Buddhas and two Bodhisattvas. The hem of the Buddha's robe is painted with tens of thousands of characters ("卍"). Thousands of Buddhas are painted around the niche. Under the shrine, the Song Dynasty painted to pay tribute to the Bodhisattva. There is a floor of the central painting on the east wall gate. Under the picture, there is one side of the Song Dynasty inscription (blurred). On the north side of the list, there are two secular male supporters of the Song Dynasty. On the south side of the list, there is a secular female supporter of the Song Dynasty (fuzzy) and a monk. In the upper part of the south of the door, there are thousands of Buddhas painted in the Sui Dynasty, and in the lower part, there are six male offerings in the Song Dynasty. Along the door, there is a banner painted in the Song Dynasty. In the upper part of the north of the gate, there are thousands of Buddhas painted in the Sui Dynasty, and in the lower part of the Song Dynasty, there are four female offerings and Bodhisattvas. Along the door, there is a banner painted in the Song Dynasty. The corridor has been repainted in the Western Xia Dynasty. The centre of the top is painted with group flowers, the south and north are painted with hanging curtains, and the south and north walls are painted to offer Bodhisattvas. The front room has been repainted by the Western Xia Dynasty, and the remaining flower patterns are covered on the top, and the murals of the Sui Dynasty are exposed at the bottom. There is a small niche on the west wall gate. There are Bodhisattvas and flowers in the niche. An orchid is painted outside the niche. There are two floors of the south and north upper parts of the door. There are offerings under the map, and the characters are blurred. The south and north walls are painted with hanging mantles and Xiangguang, and the bottom of the south wall exposes some Song paintings to support people.
