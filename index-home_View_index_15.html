Mogao Grotto 257（Nine-colour Deer Bensheng Main Room West Wall）

Views :
Update time : 2026-02-27

This Northern Wei Dynasty "Nine-colour Deer Bensheng" comes from the 259th Grotto of Mogao Grottoes, which is a classic of early murals in Dunhuang. It tells a Buddhist story about the retribution of good and evil in a segmented composition: a deer covered in nine colours of fur saved a snake man who accidentally fell into the water and told him not to reveal his whereabouts. However, when the queen dreamed of a nine-colour deer and wanted to use its fur as a decoration, the snake-playing man saw the profit and forgot the righteousness, informed the king and led the way to arrest him. At the critical juncture, the nine-colour deer told the king about his good deeds and betrayal. In the end, the king was deeply moved and ordered the protection of the nine-colour deer, and the treacherous snake man also got the retribution he deserved. The whole mural is based on vermilion, with simple and clear lines and strong colour contrasts, depicting the kindness and purity of the nine-colour deer and the greed and ugliness of the snake-artist. It is an artistic model of promoting the idea of "good and evil are rewarded".
