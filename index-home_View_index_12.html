Short Description:

Product Detail

Product Tags

Weaving and embroidery is a very representative intangible cultural heritage in China. It covers the four famous embroidery of Suzhou embroidery, Hunan embroidery, Shu embroidery and Guangdong embroidery, as well as ethnic and regional skills such as Yunjin, Song brocade, Miao embroidery and Zhuang brocade. It carries oriental aesthetics, folk traditions and craftsmanship, and is an important component of the world's intangible cultural system.

Under the background of globalisation and digitalisation, weaving and embroidery non-heritage realises international communication and lively inheritance through digital media technology,With high-definition digital files and online digital exhibitions, we can break down geographical and language barriers and let traditional weaving and embroidery patterns, crafts and aesthetics go to the global stage.


  1. Plain gold full of embroidered cloud gold dragon pattern robe material

    Craft: With the technique of full embroidery of gold, the main body of the golden dragon is embroidered with a gold thread plate, supplemented by  colourful silk thread to embroider cloud patterns and seawater river cliffs. The needles are fine and dense, and the gold thread is full of lustre.

    Culture: The dragon pattern is the exclusive royal pattern of the Qing Dynasty. The cloud pattern and the sea river cliff symbolise the stability of the imperial power and the permanence of the rivers and mountains. It is the core fabric of the court robes in the Qing Dynasty.

  2. Green satin embroidered osmanthus rabbit golden ball pattern flower god clothes

    Craft: On the green satin floor, osmanthus rabbits, leather ball flowers and other patterns are embroidered with Su embroidery techniques. The      colour matching is fresh and elegant. The rabbit pattern and osmanthus echo the image of the Mid-Autumn Festival, and the leather ball flower reflects the decorative aesthetics of the Qing Dynasty.

    Culture: The flower god clothes are special costumes for the Qing Dynasty court to worship the flower god. The pattern and colour follow the etiquette, and have both a sense of ceremony and artistic value.

  3. Moon white silk colourful cloud gold dragon pattern men's single morning robe

    Process: Adopt the technique of "passing the warp and break the weft" of silk, with the moon white as the ground, the golden dragon and colourful cloud patterns are woven, the dragon pattern is strong, the cloud pattern is elegant, and the silk texture is delicate and transparent.

    Culture: The court robe is the court dress of officials in the Qing Dynasty. Moon white is a specific grade colour in the Qing Dynasty etiquette. The  silk craft highlights the exquisite level of court weaving in the Qianlong period.

  4. Lake-coloured silk embroidery light colour lotus gold ball life character pattern clothing

    Craft: The lotus and golden ball longevity characters are embroidered with light colours on the lake-coloured silk ground. The needlework is light    and the colour is light and elegant. The golden ball longevity character implies longevity.

    Culture: The clothes are the daily clothing fabric of the concubines in the Qing Dynasty. The patterns and colours reflect the soft and elegant style of the court aesthetics in the Guangxu period.

  5. Dark green lotus eight treasure peony pattern makeup satin

    Craft: The makeup flower satin is woven with the technique of "digging flower makeup colour", with dark green as the ground, lotus, eight treasure and peony patterns rich in colour, clear layers, and the satin surface is shiny and shiny.

    Culture: Makeup flower satin is a representative category of Nanjing Yunjin. The eight-treasure pattern symbolises auspiciousness, and the peony   implies wealth. It is a high-grade fabric commonly used in the court and nobles in the Qing Dynasty.

  6. Red three-multi-hook lotus pattern makeup satin

    Craft: With red as the ground, the makeup flower technique is woven into three more (peach, pomegranate, Buddha's hand), the lotus pattern, the  colour is strong and full, the pattern is complicated and gorgeous, and the satin texture is heavy.

    Culture: Three patterns imply many blessings, longevity and many children. The lotus pattern symbolises purity and auspiciousness. It is the first    choice of fabric for weddings, festivals and other important occasions in the Qing Dynasty.

  7. Silk Buddha's hand double bird axis

    Process: The silk technique reproduces calligraphy and painting, weaving Buddha's hands, double birds, lake stones and flowers with light colours. The lines are delicate, the halo colour is natural, and it has the interest of painting and the texture of silk.

    Culture: During the Qianlong period, silk art reached its peak. Such works are often used as court displays or rewards, reflecting the artistic pursuit of "picturesque silk".
