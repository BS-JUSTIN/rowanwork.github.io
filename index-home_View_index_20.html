Mogao Grotto No. 322（Early Tang Dynasty）

Views :
Update time : 2026-02-27

The 322nd Grotto of Mogao Grottoes was built in the early Tang Dynasty and rebuilt in five generations. It is a small hall-style cave in the shape of a bucket. There is a double-layer niche on the west wall of the main room, with a Buddha, two disciples, two Bodhisattvas and two heavenly kings, with a total of seven statues. The king is like a high nose with wide wings, thick eyebrows and big eyes, similar to the image of the Hu people. On the top of the cave, the algae well is painted with branches and grape patterns in the centre of the well, and the hanging corner curtain is spread on all sides. The outermost layer of the well centre is painted with sixteen flying bodies, flying around the algae well. Thousands of Buddhas are painted on the top of the cave. The junction between the four slings and the four walls is inlaid with vine branches. Thousands of Buddhas are painted on the south wall, and there is a picture of Maitreya in the centre of the thousand Buddhas. Thousands of Buddhas are painted on the north wall, and Amitabha Sutra is painted in the centre of the thousand Buddhas. On the east wall gate, there are three paintings of the saying map; on the south side of the door, there is a picture of the pharmacist saying. On the north side is a standing statue of a disciple, surrounded by four supporters. Along the door, the five generations of paintings in the north and south are painted to pay tribute to Bodhisattvas. In the centre of the top of the road, there are five generations of paintings and sitting Buddhas, and the south and north are painted with knots and sitting Buddhas, each with two bodies.
