Mogao Grotto 323（Main Room North Wall）

Views :
Update time : 2026-02-27

This "Zhang Qian's Western Envoy Map" is a representative of Buddhist historical paintings in the early Tang Dynasty. Although it is based on history, it integrates the narrative of Buddhist association. The mural takes the landscape as the background, and the core plot is connected with a segmented composition: in the upper right corner of the Ganquan Palace, Emperor Wu of the Han Dynasty led his ministers to kneel to worship two "golden people" seized from the Xiongnu. Because he did not know their names, he decided to send Zhang Qian to the western region to explore the root cause; the lower part of the picture is the core scene of the story. Zhang Qian knelt down to accept orders with a scelboard. Han Emperor Wu rode a horse to say goodbye, and the attendants led the horse to accompany him, showing the solemnity of the envoy. In the upper left corner, Zhang Qian and his party crossed the mountains to the Daxia Kingdom. When they saw the pagoda and monks, they finally learned that the "Golden Man" was actually Buddha statue. Although the painting mistakenly marked Emperor Wu of the Han Dynasty as Emperor Xuan of the Han Dynasty, and the military diplomatic purpose of Zhang Qian's envoy was rewritten as "seeking Buddha for fame", it restored the scene of the mission's journey to the west with delicate brushstrokes, which is not only the artistic expression of Buddhism in the early Tang Dynasty, but also a precious image of witnessing the civilisation exchange of the Silk Road.
