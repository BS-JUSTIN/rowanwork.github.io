Mogao Grotto No. 254（Northern Wei Dynasty)

Views :
Update time : 2026-02-27

The 254th cave was built in the Northern Wei Dynasty. The cave is located in the middle of the cliff of Mogao Grottoes, which is the earliest central tower-column cave in Mogao Grottoes. The front of the cave is a human-shaped roof, and the north and south ends of the human-shaped roof imitate the traditional Chinese-style wooden architectural form, supported by wooden bucket arches. There are several raised rafters on the east and west sides, and the rafters are decorated with the Tianren pattern of holding lotus and winter. The back of the cave is a flat top, with a square centre tower column in the centre, connecting the top of the cave and the ground. The centre column has a niche on all sides. There is a large shrine facing the east, and the statue of Maitreya Buddha is carved inside. There were two Bodhisattvas on both sides of the Buddha. Now there is one body left on the south side and two bodies on the north side. In the shrine, the flame pattern on both sides of the head light and the body light are painted to support the Bodhisattva, and under which the Buddha's bush fairy and the deer head Brahma are painted respectively. The painting on the top of the shrine is flying to the sky. The niche is decorated with a lotus-shaped boy, a dragon-shaped niche beam, and a silk pillar on both sides. Most of the shadow sculptures on the shrine and on both sides are damaged, and only a few remain. There are two niches on the north, south and west sides of the central tower column; the north-south surface layer is a que-shaped niche, and the inner layer is a statue of Maitreya Bodhisattva; the lower layer is a round arched niche, and the inner layer is a Zen Buddha statue. On the surface facing west is a double niche, and the lower layer is a round arch niche. The statues are all meditation Buddhas sitting on knots. Outside the shrine, there is also a statue of a coercing Bodhisattva. There are shadow statues on both sides of the upper niche, but they have been blackened. The lower part of the central column, that is, the tower column, has a statue of a medicine fork on all sides. The north and south walls each have a que-shaped niche under the front of the human characters, and the inner sculpture is the cross-footed Maitreya Bodhisattva. There are four round arched niches at the upper ends of the north and south walls in the second half of the cave. There are two Buddha statues and two meditation statues each. Among them, the heads of the Buddha statues in the four shrines on the south wall are intact, and two bodies are still intact on the north wall. On the top floor of the four walls of the cave, Tiangong Jile is painted around the cave. In the middle section, a large number of thousands of Buddhas are painted. The names of the Buddhas are marked with lists, with a total of 1,235 thousand Buddhas. The position of the west side of the north and south walls opposite the central column is drawn in a diagram. In the centre of the west wall, there is a picture of the Buddha in white. In the middle part of the north and south walls of the front of the cave, there are stories of Bensheng and Buddhist stories. Under the que-shaped niche in the front of the south wall, there is a demon transformation, which is adjacent to the west side of the demon transformation. The picture book depicts the story of Saqi giving up his body and feeding the tiger. Under the front of the north wall, there is a large-scale story of fate, and the story of Nanduo's birth to be a monk, and the west side depicts the story of King Bensheng. Below the thousand Buddhas, narrative diagrams and story paintings painted on the four walls of the cave, there is a continuous decorative belt formed by geometric patterns such as square patterns and diamond patterns and plant patterns such as deformed lotus and branch winter patterns. On the bottom layer of the four walls, that is, under the decorative belt, there are several medicinal fork statues. Above the door of the east wall of the cave, there is a square window. Thousands of Buddhas are painted on the north and south sides of the door and window. The remnants of the paintings of the Sui Dynasty on the north wall of the cave are a picture.
