Enamelware is a national intangible cultural heritage integrating metal craft, glaze firing and painting art. It is most famous for Jingtai Blue, which is abrilliant treasure of traditional Chinese crafts. The Palace Museum has more than 6000 enamel cultural relics of the Yuan, Ming and Qing Dunasties, showing the beauty of the integration of court skills and Eastern and Western cultures.

Relying on digital media techology, through high-defintion images, online exhobition halls and interactive displays, enamel heavy weapons can break through the time and space restrictions, realise accurate restoration and peranment preservation, and revive ancient intangible cultural heritage in the digital age.

     3. Qing, imitation Jingtai style, silk enamel, branch-tangled lotus-patterned plum bottle

         The Qing Dynasty imitated the Jingtai style of the Ming Dynasty, and the bottle-shaped enamel was dignified. The whole body is based on sky-blue enamel, decorated with lotus patterns, full composition and rich glaze colour. Although the "Jingtai annual system" is signed, the craftsmanship is excellent, which intuitively reflects the Qing Dynasty's respect and inheritance of the Jingtai blue craft in the Ming Dynasty.

     4. In the middle of the Ming Dynasty, Jingtai's silk enamel lotus holder miscellaneous treasure pattern ear smoker

The stable shape, simple pattern and calm glaze colour are important evidences of the silk enamel technology in the middle of the Ming Dynasty.

     5. Ming Jingtai style silk enamel twig lotus pattern lion ear drum smoker

     6. Qing Qianlong Pinched Enamel Flying Dragon Pattern Honour
