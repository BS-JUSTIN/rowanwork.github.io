Mogao Grotto No. 285 (Western Wei Dynasty)

Views :
Update time : 2026-02-27

The cave was excavated during the Datong period of the Western Wei Dynasty (the fourth year of Datong of the Western Wei Dynasty (538) and the fifth   year (539 years) exist in the text of the main wall). It is the earliest cave with the exact excavation date in the Dunhuang Grottoes. The main room is a covered bucket-shaped cave, and the plane is square. In the centre of the cave, there is a square altar built in the Yuan Dynasty, and four small Zen rooms are symmetrically carved out on the south and north walls. In some Zen rooms or doors, there are still some pagodas of the small pagodas built in   the Yuan Dynasty. The square well in the centre of the cave is painted with a cover-type algae well. The four paintings are the images of traditional Chinese mythological gods and Buddhist guardian gods, including Mani Baozhu, Wrestler, Feitian, Lei Gong, Wufu Fear Beast, Fuxi, Nuwa, etc. In the lower part of the four cloaks, there are 36 Zen monks sitting in the mountains and in the hut. Various wild animals and hunting scenes are painted in the   mountains and forests. In the centre of the west wall, a round coupon-shaped niche is carved, with a small niche on each side. In the main niche is a sitting Buddha, and in the small niche, there are statues of Zen monks wearing wind hats. The upper part of the outer wall of the shrine is painted with the   image of the heavens, such as the sun, the moon, the stars, the Moshura heaven, the Viser New Heaven, the Dogomora Heaven, the Vinaye Gatian and the offering Bodhisattva, etc. The lower part is painted with the four heavenly kings, the grandmother's, etc., and the lower part of the shrine is painted with  winter patterns to decorate the south wall in three sections: upper, middle and lower. In the upper part, the wall painting 11 tricks and flying in the sky with musical instruments; in the middle, the wall painting of "The Cause of Five Hundred Robbers", and at the end, Shakyamuni and Duobao " two Buddhas sitting side by side" in the lower part, there are four small Zen rooms, and the walls between the Zen rooms are painted from east to west, "The Fate of the elder sister of Huaba"The Fate of the Evil Cow"  "The Fate of Shami's Suicide" and "Brah" The story painting of "The Door Shi's Body He ars the Rh". Its form of expression, or a long scroll of a monga, or a single painting. Under the story painting, there is a King Kong Wrestler. The north wall is divided into upper and lower sections. The upper part of the wall is painted with a diagram of seven shops, and there are vows and male and female sup porters under each floor. Among them, the second and seventh shops from the west can also be read and recognised. There are also 4 small Zen       rooms in the lower section, which correspond to the small Zen room on the south wall. Three sitting Buddhas are painted above the east wall gate, and large Buddhas are painted on the south and north sides of the gate. In the remnants of the top floor of the corridor, there are a Bodhisattva painted by the Tang Dynasty. In the south wall, the Tang Dynasty paintings are not empty silk and the Guanyin Sutra, and in the north wall, the Tang Dynasty paintings are painted by Ruyi Wheel Guanyin Sutra. The plane of the front chamber is horizontal and rectangular, and the top of the cave is sloping, and there are traces of Song paintings. There is a small cave on the west wall of the front room (Dunbian No. 286 Grotto). In the south of the door, there are still statues of the Song Dynasty. Paintings of the Middle and Tang Dynasty can be seen at the bottom, and the bottom is the murals of the Western Wei Dynasty. In the north of the door is the 287th cave, which is a portrait of a Song painting man offering.
