Short Description:

Square inches carry the style, and civilisation shares this lifeWith square inches as the square and flowers and trees as the words, the elegance and oriental aesthetics of the Forbidden City are condensed among them. Product Detail

Product Tags

Centennial Palace Museum Stamps Launch & Cultural and Creative Theme Exhibition Debut at the United Nations

This full sheet of 10 stamps is themed around "Flowers", featuring classic decorative patterns such as plum blossoms, sweet-scented osmanthus, and peonies selected from the cultural relics and architecture of the Palace Museum. It covers categories including paintings, furniture, ceramics, and architecture, with examples like Flowers and Birds album (Crabapple) by Yu Zhi of the Qing Dynasty and the Red Carved Lacquer Dish with Narcissus Pattern of the Yuan Dynasty.

The stamp design integrates traditional aesthetics with modern craftsmanship, not only embodying the historical profundity of the Palace Museum as a World Cultural Heritage site but also conveying the vision of "Civilizations Bloom Everywhere" through universally understandable symbolic patterns.Through the architectural beauty, cultural charm and historical spirit of the Palace Museum, this set of stamps pays tribute to the flourishing and colorful Chinese civilization.
