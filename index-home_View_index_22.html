Mogao Grotto No. 061（Five Generations）

Views :
Update time : 2026-02-27

The Gongde Grotto built by Cao Yuanzhong and his wife in the middle of the 10th century was rebuilt in the Song Dynasty. Located in the lower middle of the South District of Mogao Grottoes, it is one of the large caves in Mogao Grottoes. The bucket-shaped hall cave has a two-story table-type central altar in the centre of the cave. In Dunhuang literature, it is also known as "Wenshu Hall". A huge Huagai-style algae well is painted on the top of the cave in the main room, with the Wulian group dragon parrot in the centre. The centre of the four cloaks is painted with a picture of sayings, surrounded by thousands of Buddhas. There is a round arched concave wall on each corner of the top of the cave, and the four kings are painted inside. That is to say, in the southeast corner of the top, the eastern Titou Laizha Tianwang (mostly destroyed) is painted, the southwest corner is painted the southern Biuliuli Tianwang, the northwest corner is painted the western Bilou Bocha Tianwang, and the northeast corner is painted the northern Bishamen Tianwang. The upper part of the south wall, the north wall and the east wall are painted with a total of 11 Mahayana sutras, and the lower part is painted with Buddhist stories and the image of the supporter. Among them, the upper part of the west wall is painted with a large-scale "Wutai Mountain Map" with a length of 13 metres and a height of 3.6 metres, and the upper part of the south wall and the north wall are painted with five paintings. Under the south, west and north walls, there are also 33 continuous-screen Buddhist story paintings, with a total of 128 pictures, the total length of the whole painting is 28 and 7 metres, and 128 paintings are attached to the text list. Dozens of images and inscriptions of female supporters of the Cao family are also preserved on the east wall, south wall and north wall. The statues on the central altar were all destroyed, leaving only four lion hooves on the back screen, and the remains of the lion's tail on the back screen. There are two round seat marks on both sides of the lion's feet of the altar, two smaller seat marks on both sides and in front of the lion's footprint, and two image marks in front of both sides of the upper altar. The upper part of the east wall is painted with Wei Maqijing, and the lower part is painted with the image of the supporter. The top is covered with a six-ring flower pattern in the centre of the top, and the south and north are painted with hanging curtains. On the south and north walls of the road, there are images of the bright Buddha, the stars, the twelve heavenly palaces, monks, nuns and other images painted in the Yuan Dynasty, and there are inscriptions of Western Xia and Chinese. There are 63 caves on the south side of the eastern roof of the road, and 62 caves on the north side of the eastern roof. These two caves were built in the Sui Dynasty and were destroyed when the 61st cave was built. Now you can see these two caves at the east end of the road. In the Yuan Dynasty, Huangqing Temple was built in front of this cave and in front of the cave. Later, Wang Sulaiman of Xining rebuilt it in 1351.
