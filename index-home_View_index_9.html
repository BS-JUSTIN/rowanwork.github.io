Product Detail

Product Tags

Religious cultural relics area: with Tibetan Buddhism, Chinese Buddhism, Taoism and royal sacrificial cultural relics as the main body, and at the same time, it gathers foreign religious collections, presenting a pattern of diverse beliefs. The collection covers gold and bronze Buddha statues, pagodas,Dharma utensils, Tangka, offerings, etc ,with precious materials and exquisite craftsmanship, which not only reflect the royal rituals and court aesthetics,    but also contains foreign religious artefacts such as Islam and Christianity, reflecting ancient Chinese and foreign cultural exchanges and mutual learning from multiple civilisations. The overall system shows the historical vein of ancient Chinese religious art, multi-ethnic integration and Chinese and foreign cultural dialogue.

  1. Copper and gold-plated Shakyamuni Buddha

    Material technology: copper tire gold-plated, with the influence of Nepalese-Tibetan statue style.

    The characteristics of the statue: the snail hair is green, the face is round, wearing a right shoulder-shoulder robe, the right hand knot touches the  ground seal, the left hand knots the meditation seal, the knot sits on the lotus platform, with gorgeous backlight and multi-layer Sumi seat, reflecting the solemnity and craftsmanship of the Ming Dynasty court statue.

  2. Copper-plated gold-inlaid turquoise Vajra Bodhisattva

    Material technology: copper gold-plated, inlaid turquoise and other gemstones, for Tibetan Buddhist statues.

    Characteristics of statues: wearing a crown on the head, decorated with a lotus, both hands with wisdom fist seals (or holding a King Kong pestle   or a diamond bell), sitting on the lotus seat, turquoise inlay enhances the splendour and religious symbolism of the statue and is the representative of Tibetan Buddhist art in the court at the end of the Ming Dynasty.

  3. Silver gold-plated inlaid pine stone falun

    Material process: silver tire gold-plated, inlaid with turquoise, coral and other gemstones, with wooden base.

    Function and symbol: As one of the "Eight Treasures" of Buddhism, it symbolises the eternal transfer of Buddhism and the popularisation of doctrine. This Falun is a multi-layer spoke structure, with a treasure pattern in the centre, and the wheel edge is inlaid with gems. The craft is fine. It is a typical object of the Qing Dynasty court Buddha offering.

  4. Copper-plated gold-inlaid gemstones out of the halberd flower

    Material process: copper tire gold-plated, inlaid with lapis lazuli, turquoise, white jade and other gemstones, and the halberd imitates the bronze of Shang and Zhou Dynasties.

    Function and style: It is one of the "five offerings" of the court Buddha offering. The shape is regular and the pattern is fine, reflecting the integration style of Qing Dynasty antique and Tibetan Buddhist offerings.

  5. Gold-inlaid pine stone eight treasures

     Material process: gold tire, inlaid with turquoise, coral, lapis lazuli and other gemstones, made by silk and carving technology.

     Symbol and use: It is one of the "Eight Auspicious" offerings of Tibetan Buddhism. This piece is Falun, symbolising the eternal rotation of Buddhism. It is an important magic weapon for Buddhist activities in the court of the Qianlong Dynasty.

  6. Van Tong Yamandaga

    Statue identity: that is, the Great Virtude Vajra, the incarnation of the anger of Manjushri Bodhisattva, and an important guardian god of Tibetan Buddhism.

    Statue characteristics: nine faces, thirty-four arms, sixteen feet, wearing a skull, stepping on animals and demons, powerful shape, reflecting the typical style of Tibetan Buddhist statues in the early Qing Dynasty.

  7. Pinch enamel Dawei Detan City

    Material technology: copper tire pinched enamel (Jingtai blue), inlaid gemstones, complicated structure, round chassis, square supply platform, and the construction of a palace-style altar city.

    Religious function: It is the Dharma palace of Daweide Vajra, which is used for tantric consualisation and practice. It is a model of the combination of court enamel craft and Tibetan Buddhist art in the Qing Dynasty.

  8. Qianlong style carved seven Buddha patterns inlaid with gold-plated mouth inlaid gemstone conch

    Material technology: with sorganth as the foetus, gold-plated at the mouth, inlaid with coral, turquoise and other gems, spiral body relief seven Buddla statues and lotus petal pattern.

    Religious significance: It is one of the "Eight Treasures" of Tibetan Buddhism. It symbolises the spread of Miaoyin. It is engraved with Qianlong imperial poems. It is an important magic weapon in front of the Buddha in the Qing Dynasty.
