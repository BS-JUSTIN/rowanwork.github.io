Yulin Grotto No. 003（Xixia）

Views :
Update time : 2026-02-27

It was built in the late period of Guazhou during the Western Xia Dynasty. The cave contains the contents and art of Han and Tibetan nationalities, Xianzong and Tang Buddhism. It is the most mature and typical cave of art during this period, with unique characteristics. The cave is rectangular with a dome top. At the back of the centre of the cave, there is an octagonal three-level mandala (altar city), and there are several Qing sculptures on it. The lower part of the four walls of the cave is a double-layer platform in the Qing Dynasty, and there are eighteen statues of Luohan on it. The top is painted with mandala, the middle is painted with Buddha in five directions, and the centre of the east wall is painted with Buddha's biography. The north and south sides are divided into Hanmi's fifty-one thousand-hand and thousand-eyed Guanyin mandala, the foetal Tibetan mandala and Xianzongtian's scriptures. From the east of the south wall, draw a mandala, watch the immeasurable life sutra change, and a mandala each. From the east of the north wall, there are mandalas, Pure Land Change and Mandalas. The upper part of the west wall gate is painted with the Weimo change, and the north and south sides of the gate are painted with the universal sage change and the Manjushri change. The north and south walls of the corridor are painted to support the people.
